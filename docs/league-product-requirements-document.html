01The problem

Irish grassroots sport runs on volunteers, spreadsheets, and WhatsApp — and the tools built to fix that have settled around serving the club's membership and payments, not the people running the league itself or the coaches trying to develop players over years.

Every week, hundreds of amateur GAA and soccer leagues across Ireland are organised by a volunteer manually maintaining a league table, chasing captains for results over text, and recalculating standings by hand. At the same time, clubs developing young players from U8 through to adult level keep almost no structured record of a player's journey — appearances, position, involvement, development — despite this being exactly the information that helps a club retain players and identify who's ready for representative trials.

We lead with GAA. The grassroots GAA league space is less saturated than soccer, the GAA's county-board structure is a ready-made distribution wedge with no soccer equivalent, and the GAA's goals-and-points scoring is the differentiated feature. The platform stays sport-agnostic and soccer is a fast-follow.

Existing platforms (ClubZap, Pitchero, ClubSpot, LeagueRepublic, SportLoMo) are built around club membership, payments, registration, and communications — and skew toward soccer. The league table is a secondary feature, and long-term player development data isn't meaningfully addressed by any of them at the grassroots level.

02Two surfaces, two buyers

One platform, two priced surfaces sold to two different people, because they solve different problems and have different willingness to pay. They share one data model — the link is the team and the match — so the free surface feeds the paid one.

Product 1 · the funnel
League in a Box

Sold to: the volunteer running a league — a county secretary, a 5-a-side organiser, an SFAI division coordinator.

Job to be done: replace the spreadsheet with a live, shareable league table and a zero-friction result submission flow for team captains.

Why it matters strategically: it's free or near-free by design. Its real value is distribution — every club playing in a league sees the product every week, which feeds the second product.

Product 2 · the business
Club Intelligence

Sold to: club management — chairperson, underage development officer, club committee.

Job to be done: give the club a structured, multi-season record of every player's match history, involvement, and development — to inform retention conversations and representative recommendations.

Why it matters strategically: clubs have actual budget (membership fees, sponsorship, lotto income) and a real reason to pay monthly. This is where the revenue case lives.

Why penetration matters more than price. Raising the price on a small base barely moves the outcome. Moving from 5% to 15% penetration within a county changes the business entirely. The GAA's own structure — county boards sitting above individual clubs — is a natural distribution wedge: one relationship with a county board can open warm introductions to every affiliated club underneath it. The set also skews toward soccer and governing-body registration; grassroots GAA league tooling is comparatively underserved — which is why we lead there.

05League in a Box — functional spec

Core features (Phase 1)

  • League creation — divisions, teams, season dates, and a per-league Scoring Format. GAA goals-and-points (e.g. 2-08) with GAA standings points (typically 2/1/0) ship first; single-integer soccer scores with 3/1/0 follow.
  • Public league page — live table, fixtures, results, no login required to view
  • Result submission — captain opens a single-use, signed Result Link (no account, app, or password) to confirm a score
  • Automatic table recalculation the moment a result is confirmed
  • Trust-based submission with organiser override (organiser can edit any result) for the pilot
  • Sponsor banner slot on the public page (organiser can sell locally)

Phase 2 additions

  • Automated fixture scheduling across shared pitches
  • Referee/umpire assignment
  • Optional opponent-confirmation window and dispute handling
  • Multi-season public archive (becomes a long-term SEO and trust asset)
  • Soccer fast-follow once the GAA flow is proven
06Club Intelligence — functional spec

Core data captured per match

  • Opposition, venue (home/away), competition/division, pitch surface
  • Formation/team structure
  • Final score, scorers, assists, and timing of scores (e.g. which quarter/half)
  • Manager's free-text match report

Core data captured per player

  • Appearance history — minutes, position, season-by-season trend
  • Scoring and assist record over time
  • Manager's notes tied to specific matches
  • A player's-own-journey view, framed as the player's journey rather than a ranking against teammates, and intended to be shareable with the player's guardian

This is the layer the prototype currently demonstrates: a squad roster, an individual player profile with a season-by-season involvement trend, and a match log. See the accompanying interactive prototype for the working version of this screen.

07Data & child-safety approach

This is the most important section for any partner evaluating this product. Player development data on minors is high-value and high-risk, and the product is deliberately structured to keep those separate. The club is the data controller and the platform is the data processor, governed by a data processing agreement.

Context. Ireland's digital age of consent is 16; processing a child's personal data generally requires parental consent below that age, and the Irish Data Protection Commission explicitly flags sports clubs as organisations likely to be accessed by children, triggering extra obligations. The DPC has shown real enforcement appetite against far larger organisations over exactly this category of risk.

Feature tiers, by risk

LOW RISK

Team & match-level record

Formation, scorers, results, surface, division, manager's report. No individual ranking. Safe to build first, for any age group.

BUILD WITH CARE

Individual appearance history

Minutes, position, involvement trend over seasons. Framed as a player's own journey, not a comparison against teammates. Visible to coaches and, ideally, the player's own guardian.

CONSENT-GATED

Ratings & trial/tournament recommendations

The highest-value feature for club management, and the highest-risk for minors. Internal to the club only, never visible publicly or to other clubs, and disabled per player until explicit guardian consent is on file. Requires a formal data protection review before broad rollout.

The build sequencing follows this risk order: ship the low-risk tier broadly first, ship the care tier for adult and consenting youth players, and hold the consent-gated tier behind a legal review and a single pilot club before wider release.

Consent model. Per-player, revocable guardian consent (not a single blanket club-registration checkbox) unlocks the consent-gated tier for that player. Ordinary appearance history relies on the club's own lawful basis as controller. Right-to-erasure is honoured; on club churn, data is exported then deleted after a grace period — specifics to be set by the data-protection review.

League pricing is intentionally low — its function is adoption and distribution, not margin. Club Intelligence carries the actual revenue case, priced against what a club committee can reasonably approve as a recurring software line, comparable to existing club software spend already in the market.

09Roadmap
1

Build & pilot League in a Box — GAA-first

Launch with one or two real GAA leagues, shipping the goals-and-points scoring format from day one. Validate the captain-submission flow and table-sharing behaviour in practice. Soccer follows once the GAA flow is proven.

2

Layer in Club Intelligence, low-risk tier only

Team and match-level record, offered to clubs already inside a piloted league. No individual ratings yet.

3

Data protection review

Formal review of consent flows, retention, and deletion policy ahead of enabling individual ratings and trial recommendations.

4

County-board led expansion

Approach county boards directly as a distribution wedge into their affiliated club base, rather than selling club by club.

10Open questions for partners
  • Does a partner have existing relationships with GAA county boards or FAI-affiliated leagues that could shortcut the pilot stage?
  • Confirm the legal structure for guardian consent capture in the data-protection review (working decision: per-player, per-feature for the consent-gated tier).
  • Should Club Intelligence integrate with existing club registration systems (e.g. Foireann), or remain fully standalone?
  • Is there appetite to pilot the consent-gated ratings tier with a single trusted club before broader rollout?
