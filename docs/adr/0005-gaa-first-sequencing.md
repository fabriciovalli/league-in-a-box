# GAA-first, soccer as fast-follow

The pilot and positioning lead with GAA rather than soccer, while the platform stays sport-agnostic and
soccer remains a fast-follow. We reversed the drafts' "soccer-first" line because its stated rationale
(lower data sensitivity) was misapplied — the league surface holds almost no personal data, and the
data-sensitivity risk lives in Club Intelligence's consent-gated tier, which is gated behind a
data-protection review regardless of sport. GAA-first is more consistent with the rest of the strategy:
the county-board distribution wedge is a GAA-native structure with no soccer equivalent, the GAA
goals-and-points Scoring Format is the differentiated feature, and the grassroots GAA league space is
less saturated than soccer (LeagueRepublic/SportLoMo skew soccer and registration).

Trade-offs accepted: the goals-and-points Scoring Format and GAA Standings rules (e.g. 2/1/0) must ship
in Phase 1 rather than later; marketing must handle GAA trademark sensitivity (the platform is not
affiliated with the GAA); county-board gatekeeping is slower to land than an independent soccer
organiser; and Club Intelligence's higher-sensitivity underage path comes first (contained by the
consent gate). Recorded because it reverses an explicit earlier decision and shapes Phase 1 build,
positioning, and go-to-market.
